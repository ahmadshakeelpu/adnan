About Me

A scientist who loves teaching

I believe every student has a moment when physics transforms from confusing symbols into a language for understanding reality. My job is to engineer that moment.

I graduated with a BS (Hons) in Physics from Government College University Faisalabad (GCUF) in 2023. Since then I have been teaching Physics to Class 11 and 12 students at Punjab College Sillanwali, running both in-person classes and online sessions simultaneously.

Although I am not B.Ed qualified or Cambridge certified, I teach the Cambridge AS & A-Level Physics syllabus (9702) in full — covering all topics, past-paper practice, and exam technique that the syllabus demands. My focus is on building genuine understanding, not just rote exam preparation.

In two years of teaching I have worked with students across a range of abilities and helped many make significant improvements in their exam performance. I continue to develop my own subject knowledge and teaching skills every day.

Teaching Philosophy

How I think about physics education

Five principles that shape every lesson I design and deliver.

"Physics is not a collection of facts to memorise — it is a way of asking questions that nature can actually answer."
01

Build mental models first

Equations describe reality — they don't replace understanding it. I introduce every topic with a concrete image or analogy before introducing symbols.

02

Questions over answers

Lessons are designed to provoke curiosity. I celebrate a student's great question more loudly than a correct answer.

03

Deliberate practice

Past-paper work is strategic, not random. Each problem set targets a specific misconception identified from formative assessment.

04

Low-stakes experimentation

Students must feel safe to be wrong. My classroom culture treats errors as data — the most useful kind.

05

Accessibility by design

Materials are multilingual (English/Urdu), screen-reader friendly, and usable on low-bandwidth connections.
